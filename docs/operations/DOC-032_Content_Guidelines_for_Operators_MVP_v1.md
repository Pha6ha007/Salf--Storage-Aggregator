# 1. Document Role & Scope

## 1.1. Purpose

This document establishes **baseline content quality expectations** for operators who create and maintain listings on the Self-Storage Aggregator platform.

**What this document provides:**
- Clear, practical guidance for creating helpful, accurate content
- Principles for maintaining content quality and user trust
- Understanding of what content supports positive user experience
- Collaborative framework for content improvement

**What this document is NOT:**
- ❌ A legal contract or Terms of Service
- ❌ An enforcement policy with automatic sanctions
- ❌ A detailed moderation playbook with specific penalties
- ❌ A marketing strategy or SEO optimization guide
- ❌ A guarantee of content approval or listing promotion

## 1.2. Scope (MVP v1)

**Content Types Covered:**
- Warehouse descriptions (name, address, general description)
- Box unit descriptions (dimensions, features, notes)
- Facility photographs and visual media
- Amenity declarations (climate control, security, access hours, etc.)
- Pricing-related descriptions (context, notes, special conditions)

**Out of Scope:**
- User-generated content (reviews, ratings) - covered by separate Trust & Safety policies
- Operator account information - covered by Legal Documentation (DOC-054)
- Promotional campaigns or advertisements - not part of MVP v1
- Third-party integrations or external content - not part of MVP v1

## 1.3. Philosophy & Approach

**Principle-Driven, Not Punitive:**

This document operates on the belief that **most operators want to provide accurate, helpful information** and benefit from clear guidance rather than strict enforcement.

**Core Philosophy:**
- **Education over punishment** - Help operators understand what works
- **Collaboration over control** - Work with operators to improve content
- **Clarity over complexity** - Simple, understandable expectations
- **Context over rules** - Recognize that content needs vary by facility

**Enforcement Approach:**

The platform may review content and may contact operators about content that does not meet these guidelines. This is a **collaborative, educational process**, not an automatic penalty system.

See Section 7 for details on how platform feedback works in practice.

# 2. General Content Principles

These principles apply to all content created by operators on the platform.

## 2.1. Accuracy

**Principle:** Content should truthfully represent the facility, its features, and its services.

**What this means:**
- Physical descriptions match the actual facility
- Amenities listed are actually available and functional
- Location information is correct and verifiable
- Operational details (hours, access, policies) reflect current reality

**Why it matters:**
- Users make decisions based on this information
- Inaccurate content damages user trust and operator reputation
- Misleading information may expose operators to liability
- Accurate content reduces disputes and support requests

**Examples of Accurate Content:**
- "Climate-controlled units maintain 15-25°C year-round" (if this is true)
- "24/7 access via electronic keypad entry" (if facility has this)
- "Located in Taganskaya district, 10 minutes walk from metro station" (if accurate)

**Examples of Inaccurate Content:**
- Claiming 24/7 access when facility has restricted hours
- Listing amenities that are not functional or not available
- Using photos of other facilities or stock images
- Describing boxes as "spacious" when they are below-average size

## 2.2. Clarity

**Principle:** Content should be easy to understand and helpful to users making decisions.

**What this means:**
- Descriptions use straightforward language
- Important information is not buried or obscured
- Technical terms are explained when necessary
- Key facility features are prominently described

**Why it matters:**
- Users need clear information to make confident booking decisions
- Unclear content increases user confusion and support inquiries
- Clear descriptions improve conversion and reduce cancellations

**Good Practices:**
- Use plain language appropriate for a general audience
- Structure information logically (facility overview, then specific details)
- Highlight key features that differentiate the facility
- Avoid jargon unless it's standard industry terminology

**Examples of Clear Content:**
- "Small box (2m × 1.5m × 2m) suitable for seasonal clothing, sports equipment, or office supplies"
- "Security includes 24/7 CCTV monitoring and individual unit alarms"

**Examples of Unclear Content:**
- Using only technical specifications without context ("SRC-45B unit configuration")
- Vague descriptions ("nice facility," "good location," "affordable storage")
- Leaving critical information unstated (e.g., not mentioning limited access hours)

## 2.3. Honesty

**Principle:** Content should not mislead, deceive, or make false promises.

**What this means:**
- Do not exaggerate facility features or quality
- Do not make claims that cannot be verified
- Do not use manipulative language to pressure bookings
- Be transparent about limitations or conditions

**Why it matters:**
- Trust is the foundation of marketplace success
- Dishonest content creates negative user experiences
- Misleading users damages operator reputation and platform credibility
- Transparency builds long-term customer relationships

**Examples of Honest Content:**
- "Basic facility with essential security features - no climate control available"
- "Access available Mon-Sat 7am-10pm; closed Sundays"
- "Older facility (built 2005) but well-maintained with regular inspections"

**Examples of Dishonest Content:**
- "Best storage facility in Moscow!" (unverifiable superlative)
- "Never had a single security incident!" (impossible to verify)
- Hiding significant limitations or restrictions
- Making promises the operator cannot keep ("guaranteed 100% availability")

## 2.4. Relevance

**Principle:** Content should be pertinent to users' storage decisions and facility evaluation.

**What this means:**
- Descriptions focus on practical information users need
- Irrelevant personal opinions or commentary are minimized
- Marketing language does not overwhelm factual information
- Content is appropriate for the specific field or section

**Why it matters:**
- Users value efficiency - they want relevant information quickly
- Irrelevant content dilutes important details
- Professional, focused content builds operator credibility

**Examples of Relevant Content:**
- In "Box Features" field: dimensions, access type, climate control status, security features
- In "Warehouse Description": location details, overall facility amenities, parking availability
- In "Special Conditions": deposit requirements, access policies, booking restrictions

**Examples of Irrelevant Content:**
- Extended personal stories unrelated to facility features
- Excessive marketing slogans that don't inform users
- Off-topic commentary in technical description fields

# 3. Text Content Guidelines

These guidelines apply specifically to written descriptions, titles, and text fields.

## 3.1. Warehouse Names & Titles

**Purpose:** Warehouse names appear in search results and are the first impression users have of a facility.

**Guidelines:**
- Use the actual facility name or a clear, descriptive identifier
- Include location if helpful for user recognition
- Keep names concise and memorable (typically under 50 characters)
- Avoid excessive keywords, promotional language, or special characters

**Good Examples:**
- "Skladovka Self-Storage - Taganskaya"
- "SecureBox Moscow Central"
- "WarehouseCity Domodedovo"

**Poor Examples:**
- "⭐⭐⭐ BEST CHEAP STORAGE MOSCOW DISCOUNT 24/7 SECURE ⭐⭐⭐"
- "Amazing storage super cheap WOW! Book now!!!"
- Using only generic terms: "Storage Facility #2"

## 3.2. Warehouse Descriptions

**Purpose:** General warehouse descriptions help users understand the overall facility.

**Guidelines:**
- Start with the most important information (location, access type, security)
- Describe what makes the facility unique or distinctive
- Include practical details (parking, loading areas, facility size)
- Keep descriptions focused and scannable (200-500 words ideal)
- Use proper grammar and sentence structure

**Structure Suggestion:**
1. Location and accessibility
2. Facility type and overall size
3. Key amenities and features
4. Security and access details
5. Any unique characteristics or services

**Good Example:**
> "Modern self-storage facility located in the Taganskaya district, 10 minutes walk from Taganskaya metro station. The facility offers 150+ storage units ranging from 2m² to 20m² with 24/7 electronic access. All units feature individual alarms and the building has comprehensive CCTV coverage. Free parking available for loading and unloading. Ground floor units include drive-up access for convenient moving. Facility is clean, well-lit, and regularly maintained."

**Poor Examples:**
- Single-sentence descriptions: "We have storage boxes."
- Excessive marketing: "The absolute BEST storage in all of Russia! Unbeatable prices! Amazing service! You won't believe how good we are!"
- Irrelevant detail: "I started this business because my grandmother always told me..."

## 3.3. Box Unit Descriptions

**Purpose:** Box descriptions help users understand specific unit characteristics and suitability.

**Guidelines:**
- Always include dimensions (length × width × height in meters)
- Specify key features (climate control, ground floor, drive-up access, etc.)
- Provide context about what the unit can hold (optional but helpful)
- Mention any unit-specific restrictions or requirements

**Good Examples:**
- "2m × 1.5m × 2m (6m³) - Climate-controlled unit suitable for furniture, electronics, or documents. Ground floor with cart access."
- "4m × 3m × 2.5m (30m³) - Large unit with drive-up access. Ideal for moving or business inventory storage."

**Poor Examples:**
- Missing dimensions: "Medium-sized box"
- Vague descriptions: "Nice unit, good for stuff"
- Incorrect dimensions or misleading size claims

## 3.4. Amenity Descriptions

**Purpose:** Amenity information helps users filter and compare facilities.

**Guidelines:**
- Only declare amenities that are actually available and functional
- Be honest about amenity quality and accessibility
- If amenities have limitations, mention them
- Update amenity status if conditions change

**Examples:**
- "Climate Control: Yes - All units maintain 15-25°C year-round"
- "Security: 24/7 CCTV, individual unit alarms, secure perimeter fencing"
- "Access Hours: Mon-Sat 7am-10pm; Closed Sundays"
- "Parking: Yes - 10 spaces available for loading/unloading"

**What to avoid:**
- Declaring amenities that don't exist
- Overstating amenity quality ("military-grade security" for basic locks)
- Not updating when amenities become unavailable

## 3.5. Language & Grammar

**Guidelines:**
- Use standard Russian language (platform's primary language for MVP v1)
- Check spelling and grammar - errors reduce credibility
- Avoid excessive capitalization, punctuation, or special characters
- Keep formatting simple and readable

**Note on Language:**
- MVP v1 operates in Russian language
- English content may be supported in future versions
- Machine translation is not recommended for official descriptions

# 4. Image & Media Guidelines

Visual content is critical for user trust and decision-making.

## 4.1. Photo Authenticity

**Principle:** Photos must represent the actual facility being listed.

**Requirements:**
- Use real photographs of your own facility
- Photos should be current (ideally taken within the past year)
- Show actual conditions, not staged or misleading scenarios
- If facility has been renovated or changed, update photos

**Prohibited:**
- ❌ Stock photos from image libraries
- ❌ Photos of other facilities
- ❌ Heavily edited or filtered images that misrepresent reality
- ❌ Using another operator's photos without permission

**Why this matters:**
- Users expect photos to match what they will see on arrival
- Mismatched photos create disappointment and cancellations
- Authentic photos build trust and reduce disputes

## 4.2. Photo Quality Expectations

**Guidelines:**
- Photos should be reasonably clear and well-lit
- Images should be in focus and properly framed
- Minimum recommended resolution: 1024×768 pixels
- Photos should show relevant facility areas

**What to photograph:**
- Exterior view (building entrance, signage)
- Sample storage units (different sizes)
- Security features (access control, cameras - where appropriate)
- Common areas (hallways, elevators, loading zones)
- Amenities (climate control systems, lighting, cleanliness)

**Acceptable photo quality:**
- Clear smartphone photos are acceptable
- Professional photography is welcome but not required
- Natural lighting is fine; perfect staging is not expected

**What to avoid:**
- Extremely dark or blurry images
- Photos that show unrelated facilities or misleading angles
- Excessive filters or photo manipulation
- Photos that are pixelated or too small to see details

## 4.3. Photo Content Standards

**Guidelines:**
- Show clean, maintained facility conditions
- Present facilities honestly, including any visible wear
- Avoid photos that show safety hazards or poor conditions
- Ensure photos do not include personal information or faces of individuals without consent

**Privacy Considerations:**
- Do not show other customers or their belongings
- Blur or exclude license plates or personal identifiers
- Do not photograph areas that compromise security (e.g., full keypad codes)

## 4.4. Minimum Photo Requirements

**MVP v1 Requirements:**
- At least 1 photo per warehouse (exterior or interior)
- Recommended: 3-5 photos showing different aspects of facility
- Photos should be uploaded through the platform interface

**Note:** Photo moderation in MVP v1 is manual and reactive. Operators are expected to self-regulate photo quality and authenticity.

# 5. Prohibited Content

The following content is not acceptable on the platform.

## 5.1. False or Deceptive Information

**Prohibited:**
- Factually incorrect descriptions of facilities, features, or services
- Claiming amenities that are not available
- Misleading size or capacity information
- False claims about location, access, or availability
- Fabricated reviews or testimonials

**Why:** Deceptive content harms users, violates trust principles, and may expose operators to legal liability.

## 5.2. Discriminatory or Offensive Content

**Prohibited:**
- Language that discriminates based on race, ethnicity, religion, gender, disability, or other protected characteristics
- Offensive, vulgar, or inappropriate language
- Content that promotes intolerance or hostility
- Discriminatory policies or restrictions (e.g., "No [specific group] allowed")

**Why:** The platform is committed to fair, inclusive marketplace practices. Discriminatory content violates legal requirements and platform values.

**Legal Context:** See DOC-054 (Legal Checklist) § 1.2 for Terms of Service requirements regarding non-discrimination.

## 5.3. Illegal or Unsafe Use Claims

**Prohibited:**
- Suggesting storage can be used for illegal activities
- Promoting unsafe storage of hazardous materials
- Encouraging violation of storage regulations or safety codes
- Suggesting living or habitation in storage units (where prohibited)

**Why:** Platform has responsibility to promote safe, legal use. Content suggesting illegal use creates liability risk.

**Examples of prohibited statements:**
- "Perfect for storing anything - no questions asked!"
- "Great place to keep items you don't want authorities to find"
- "You can store chemicals, explosives, anything you need"

## 5.4. Spam or Irrelevant Content

**Prohibited:**
- Keyword stuffing or repetitive text designed to manipulate search
- Irrelevant external links or advertisements
- Promotional content for unrelated services
- Excessive capitalization, symbols, or emojis that reduce readability

**Examples:**
- "STORAGE STORAGE STORAGE MOSCOW STORAGE CHEAP CHEAP STORAGE MOSCOW STORAGE"
- Embedding links to unrelated businesses or competitors
- Using description fields to advertise unrelated services

**Why:** Spam degrades user experience, reduces content quality, and makes the platform less useful for everyone.

## 5.5. Misleading Pricing Information

**Prohibited:**
- Advertising prices that are not actually available
- Hidden fees not disclosed upfront
- "Bait and switch" tactics (advertising one price, charging another)
- False discount claims ("50% off" when no valid original price exists)

**Why:** Pricing transparency is essential for user trust and legal compliance.

**Important:** This section covers pricing descriptions in text content. Actual pricing data and calculations are handled through structured fields and API endpoints (see DOC-003 API Design Blueprint § 6 for booking price calculation logic).

# 6. Updates & Maintenance

Content should reflect current reality and be kept reasonably up-to-date.

## 6.1. When to Update Content

**Operators should update content when:**
- Facility conditions change materially (renovations, damage, closures)
- Amenities are added, removed, or become non-functional
- Access policies or operational hours change
- Pricing structure or conditions change significantly
- Photos no longer represent current facility conditions

**Examples:**
- If climate control system breaks down, update amenity status
- If facility is undergoing renovation, add note to description
- If access hours change seasonally, update access information
- If exterior or interior has changed significantly, update photos

## 6.2. Reasonable Update Expectations (MVP v1)

**What "reasonable" means:**
- Major changes should be updated within 1-2 weeks
- Critical changes (facility closure, safety issues) should be updated immediately
- Minor updates (refreshing photos, improving descriptions) can happen quarterly
- Routine maintenance does not require immediate content updates

**What is NOT expected:**
- Daily or weekly content updates for minor details
- Professional content rewriting or editing services
- Immediate response to all platform feedback (see Section 7)

**Realistic MVP v1 Context:**
- Content updates are operator-driven in MVP v1
- Platform does not have automated content expiration or forced updates
- Operators are trusted to maintain content accuracy as a matter of professionalism

## 6.3. Content Refresh Best Practices

**Recommendations (not requirements):**
- Review content quarterly to ensure accuracy
- Update photos annually or after significant facility changes
- Revise descriptions if you notice higher-than-usual user questions about specific topics
- Improve content based on user feedback or reviews mentioning confusion

# 7. Platform Review & Feedback (Conceptual)

This section describes how the platform may engage with operator content, without creating enforcement obligations.

## 7.1. Content Review Approach

**MVP v1 Approach:**
- Content review is **manual, reactive, and human-driven**
- Platform may review content in response to:
  - User reports or complaints
  - Support tickets indicating content confusion
  - Routine quality checks during onboarding
  - Anomaly detection (if implemented in future phases)

**What content review is NOT:**
- ❌ Not automated approval/rejection system
- ❌ Not 24/7 real-time moderation
- ❌ Not guaranteed response time or SLA
- ❌ Not enforcement with penalties

**Review Philosophy:**
- Reviews are collaborative, not adversarial
- Goal is to help operators improve, not to penalize
- Context matters - reviewers consider operator intent and facility circumstances

## 7.2. If Content Needs Improvement

**What happens:**

If platform team identifies content that does not meet these guidelines, the operator may be contacted with:
- Specific description of the issue
- Explanation of why the content is problematic
- Suggestions for improvement
- Reasonable timeframe to make updates (typically 1-2 weeks for non-urgent issues)

**Example scenarios:**
- "We noticed your warehouse description mentions '24/7 access' but booking conditions specify 'access by appointment only.' Could you clarify or update the description to avoid user confusion?"
- "Some of your photos appear to be stock images rather than your actual facility. We recommend replacing them with real photos to build user trust."

**Operator responsibilities:**
- Respond to platform feedback in reasonable timeframe
- Make good-faith effort to address identified issues
- Ask questions if guidance is unclear

**Important:** This is a collaborative process. Operators are not penalized for needing to improve content - everyone starts somewhere.

## 7.3. No Automatic Sanctions (MVP v1)

**Critical Clarification:**

This document does **NOT** establish automatic sanctions, penalties, or enforcement actions for content issues.

**What this means:**
- Content that needs improvement does not automatically result in account suspension
- Operators are not fined or penalized for content that is unclear or needs updating
- Platform works with operators to improve content over time

**Escalation only for serious violations:**

In rare cases of **deliberately deceptive, illegal, or harmful content** (see Section 5), the platform may need to take action to protect users. However:
- Such actions require human review and judgment
- Operators are contacted and given opportunity to explain
- Decisions are made in context, not automatically

**Separate processes:**

Trust & Safety violations (fraud, discrimination, illegal activity) are handled through separate processes defined in:
- DOC-106: Trust & Safety Framework
- Legal terms and conditions (DOC-054)

These are distinct from routine content quality guidance.

## 7.4. Continuous Improvement Mindset

**Platform commitment:**

The platform is learning alongside operators in MVP v1. We recognize:
- Content guidelines evolve based on real-world experience
- What works for users may not be immediately obvious
- Operators have valuable insights about their facilities

**Feedback loop:**

Operators are encouraged to provide feedback on these guidelines:
- Are any expectations unclear or unrealistic?
- Do certain guidelines not fit your facility type?
- What additional guidance would be helpful?

The platform will iterate on these guidelines based on operator and user feedback.

**Future possibilities** (not commitments):

# 10. Risks & Trade-offs

This section acknowledges inherent tensions in content policy.

## 10.1. Risk: Over-Restriction

**Concern:**

Overly strict content guidelines could:
- Discourage operator participation
- Limit operator creativity and differentiation
- Create barrier to entry for smaller operators
- Make platform feel bureaucratic or unfriendly

**Mitigation in this document:**
- Guidelines are principle-based, not rule-based
- Focus on education, not enforcement
- Allow for context and operator circumstances
- No automatic penalties for content issues

**Ongoing balance:** Platform will monitor whether guidelines feel restrictive and adjust if operators report excessive friction.

## 10.2. Risk: Under-Enforcement

**Concern:**

Without strong enforcement, guidelines could be ignored, leading to:
- Low-quality content flooding the platform
- User frustration with inaccurate or misleading listings
- Competitive disadvantage for operators who follow guidelines
- Platform reputation damage

**Mitigation in this document:**
- Clear expectations set for operators
- Human review process for serious issues
- Collaborative improvement approach encourages voluntary compliance
- Trust & Safety framework provides escalation for serious violations

**Ongoing balance:** Platform will monitor content quality and user complaints. If voluntary compliance is insufficient, enforcement may need to strengthen in future versions.

## 10.3. Risk: Content Inconsistency

**Concern:**

Without standardization, content may become:
- Highly variable in quality and format
- Difficult for users to compare across facilities
- Less useful for search and filtering
- Harder to maintain at scale

**Mitigation in this document:**
- Structured fields guide content creation (warehouse name, description, amenities)
- Examples show what good content looks like
- Guidelines create common baseline expectations

**Accepted trade-off:** Some content variation is natural and even valuable - different facilities have different characteristics. Perfect standardization is not the goal.

## 10.4. Risk: Operator Friction

**Concern:**

Content guidelines could create friction in operator experience:
- Additional time burden to create compliant content
- Confusion about what is expected
- Frustration if content is rejected or requires revision
- Perception of platform being difficult to work with

**Mitigation in this document:**
- Guidelines are realistic and achievable
- Education and examples provided
- Feedback is collaborative, not punitive
- Reasonable timeframes for updates

**Ongoing monitoring:** Platform will collect operator feedback on whether guidelines feel reasonable and achievable.

## Appendix B: Examples of Good vs. Poor Content

### Example 1: Warehouse Description

**GOOD:**
> "SecureBox Moscow is located in the Taganskaya district, 10 minutes walking distance from Taganskaya metro station. The facility features 120 climate-controlled units ranging from 2m² to 20m², all equipped with individual alarms. Access is available 24/7 via electronic keycard. The building includes CCTV monitoring, well-lit hallways, and loading areas with free parking. Ground floor units offer drive-up access for convenient moving. Regular maintenance and cleaning ensure a professional storage environment."

**WHY GOOD:**
- Specific location details help users decide accessibility
- Amenities are clearly listed with verifiable features
- Information is structured logically (location → features → access → benefits)
- Tone is professional and factual
- Details are specific enough to be useful

**POOR:**
> "We are THE BEST storage in all of Moscow!!!! Super cheap prices!!!! Amazing facility!!!! You will LOVE our boxes!!! Book NOW before we run out!!! 100% satisfaction GUARANTEED!!! Nobody can beat our incredible service!!!"

**WHY POOR:**
- Excessive marketing language reduces credibility
- No factual information provided
- Unverifiable claims ("THE BEST," "100% satisfaction")
- Poor grammar and excessive punctuation
- Does not help user make informed decision

### Example 2: Box Unit Description

**GOOD:**
> "Medium box - 3m × 2m × 2.5m (15m³). Climate-controlled with temperature maintained at 18-22°C year-round. Suitable for furniture from a 1-bedroom apartment, seasonal items, or small business inventory. Located on ground floor with cart access from loading area."

**WHY GOOD:**
- Exact dimensions provided
- Key features specified (climate control, temperature range)
- Practical usage examples help users assess fit
- Location detail (ground floor, cart access) is useful

**POOR:**
> "Nice medium box. Good size for your things. Very affordable. Great for storage."

**WHY POOR:**
- No dimensions or measurements
- Vague language ("nice," "good," "great")
- No useful information to help user decide
- Generic description could apply to any box
